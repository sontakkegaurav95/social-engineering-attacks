🔐 Social Engineering Attacks

📌 Definition

Social engineering attacks are cyberattacks that manipulate people into revealing confidential information instead of exploiting technical vulnerabilities .In this instead of hacking systems, attackers hack human behavior.

🧠 Key Idea

“The weakest link in security is often the human.”

Attackers use: Trust,Fear, Urgency,Curiosity to trick victims.

⚠️ Types of Social Engineering Attacks
1. Phishing
Fake emails/messages pretending to be trusted sources
Victim clicks malicious links and enters credentials

Example: Fake bank email asking for login details

2. Pre-texting
Attacker creates a fake identity Gains victim’s trust to extract information

Example: Fake IT support asking for password

3. Baiting
Luring victims using tempting offers Malware spreads through downloads or USB devices

Example: Free movie download with hidden virus

4. Tailgating (Piggybacking)
Unauthorized person follows an authorized user into restricted area .

Example: Someone entering office without ID

⚙️ How Social Engineering Works
Information gathering (about target)
Building trust
Exploiting emotions
Extracting sensitive data

💥 Impact of Social Engineering

🔓 Unauthorized access
💳 Financial loss
🕵️ Identity theft
📉 Reputation damage
🦠 Malware infection

🌍 Real-World Examples
 Google & Facebook Scam (2013–2015): Lost $100 million due to phishing
 Twitter Hack (2020): Attackers used social engineering to access internal tools

🛡️ Prevention Techniques

🔐 Technical Measures
Use Antivirus & Firewall
Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
Email spam filters

👨‍🏫 User Awareness
Do not click unknown links
Verify sender identity
Avoid sharing sensitive information

🏢 Organizational Measures
Security training programs
Strong access control
Regular audits
